About Gaia





Gaia Organic Farm was setup in 1999 by Lucia Tanase and Mark Tanase.


Gaia, our Organic Farm

Caring and
Conscientous Farming

History

Lucia and Mark are husband and wife and have known each other all their lives.The Gaia family have been farming the land around Algonquin Highlands, for 50 years. The family became involved in the market garden programme and began marketing their harvest direct to the community at local marketplaces. Lucia grew up with his mother and her other younger brothers in the farmhouse that the farm is still based in. They grew up together around the farm, as her husband Mark lived neighbouring.

Adversity and Passsion

Unfortunately, Mark's father died when he was only 17 years old. Mark grew up in times when there was absolutely no emphasis on health and safety. Mark is convinced that his father died prematurely for these reasons, given that his father worked with different chemicals that were used as a seed conditioner. Nowadays, Mark is a dedicated lawyer, dealing especially with such issues, so that these techniques are not used.

Gaia's Organic Farm is Formed

In 1999, the two spouses already had three children between the ages of 13 and 17. Lucia and Mark were passionate about eating healthy food, which they also brought to their children. Passing through these experiences and passions, the family formed the Gaia Organic Farm.Wanting to pass on the idea of eating healthy, it has given local people the opportunity to buy organic products from their farm.

As Mark already had experience in pig husbandry, they wanted to return to the traditional style of the agricultural market and started by rearing organic pork and vegetables. They quickly grew and developed their techniques. Over time, they have expanded further, growing cows, goats and fruit trees. In the beginning, sales were made at the farm gate, then they opened up their own store, selling organic pork, beef, goat products, and organic fruit and vegetables.

Today and the future

Today, Gaia is still a family business. Their children continue to help and work on the farm. Their two sons are in charge of animal care, and their daughter along with other family members, is engaged in growing and cultivating fruits and vegetables. Lucia and Mark's responsibility is the farm shop.
